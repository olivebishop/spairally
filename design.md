Website Design Audit & Improvement Prompt

You are a senior UI/UX designer and conversion specialist. Conduct a comprehensive design audit and improvement pass on this website across 5 core pillars:

1. TYPOGRAPHY

Establish a clear type scale — audit and improve font sizes across every breakpoint
Tighten letter-spacing on headings; improve readability spacing on body text
Set consistent line-height: ~1.2–1.4 for headings, ~1.6–1.8 for body
Check heading vs. body text opacity — headings 100%, body 80–90%, captions/meta 60–70%
Every typographic decision should reinforce visual hierarchy: size, weight, contrast, and alignment working together


2. LAYOUT

Audit the grid system — ensure consistent column structure and gutters
Build a strict spacing system (e.g. 4px or 8px base unit) applied consistently to padding, margins, and gaps
Enforce visual hierarchy through size contrast and intentional alignment — not everything can compete for attention
Use depth (subtle shadows, layering, z-axis) to make the UI feel tangible and three-dimensional — not flat
Apply visual rhyming — repeat shapes, radii, spacing patterns, and motifs to create rhythm and cohesion across the page
Subtle details only — no element should compete with another for dominance


3. COLOUR

Apply the 60/30/10 rule: 60% dominant neutral, 30% secondary brand tone, 10% accent for CTAs and highlights
Prioritise contrast above all — WCAG AA minimum, AAA where possible
Use colour intentionally to guide the eye through the page hierarchy
Depth through colour: use light/dark tonal shifts, not just flat fills


4. PERFORMANCE & LOAD

Flag anything that could slow perceived load: heavy images, render-blocking elements, layout shifts
Recommend lazy loading, next-gen image formats, and minimal animation overhead
Fast-loading pages are part of the design — a slow site kills trust before a word is read


5. CONVERSION
Each page must be treated as a conversion asset:

Page Goal: Every page has one primary goal. Design must serve that goal — remove anything that competes with it
CTAs: Clear, strategic, and placed where intent is highest. Use size, colour (10% accent), and whitespace to make them unmissable. CTA copy should speak to outcome, not action (e.g. "Start growing" not "Submit")
Trust: Surface social proof — testimonials, logos, numbers, results — at the exact moment doubt arises
Clarity: The value proposition must be understood in under 5 seconds. No jargon. No ambiguity
Feeling: The site must make visitors feel something — speak to real motivations, not just features. Show empathy: your clients should feel seen, understood, and not alone. This means copy and design working together to reflect their pain, their goal, and your belief in them


Guiding Principle
It's the subtle details that make the huge difference. Visual rhyming, consistent depth, quiet hierarchy — nothing competes, everything connects. The result should feel premium, intentional, and effortless to navigate.
